Organization overview

A US $40 bfadeillion corporation, the Aditya Birla Group is in the league of Fortune 500 companies. It is anchored by an extraordinary force of over 120,000 employees belonging to 42 nationalities. The Group has been ranked No. 4 in the global 'Top Companies for Leaders' survey and ranked No. 1 in the Asia Pacific for 2011. 'Top Companies for Leaders' is the most comprehensive study of organisational leadership in the world conducted by Aon Hewitt, Fortune Magazine, and RBL (a strategic HR and Leadership Advisory firm).
Over 50 percent of the Aditya Birla Group's revenues flow from its overseas operations. The Group operates in 35 countries - Australia, Austria, Bangladesh, Brazil, Canada, China, Egypt, France, Germany, India, Indonesia, Italy, Ivory Coast, Japan, Korea, Laos, Luxembourg, Malaysia, Myanmar, Philippines, Poland, Russia, Singapore, South Africa, Spain, Sri Lanka, Sweden, Switzerland, Tanzania, Thailand, Turkey, UAE, UK, USA, and Vietnam.

Program overview

Encore’ – official Comeback program for women on career breaks by ADITYA BIRLA GROUP. The intent of the program is to provide mutually rewarding work opportunities to qualified Indian women who are out of the workforce due to various reasons.
Encore’ offers live projects with flexible workplace arrangements wherever possible where the participants use their expertise to solve real business problems. You will work closely with the project sponsors of the identified projects who will also be their mentors during the course of the project.
The program is an effective interim platform that enables you to slowly transition back into a workplace environment. The participants can also subsequently look at exploring possible full-time work opportunities across ABG's Apparel & Retail businesses in consultation with their project mentors.

Who can apply?

Women who meet all of the following criteria are eligible to apply for the Program:
  • Post-graduation or relevant equivalent technical qualifications
  • Minimum work experience of two years
  • Not more than 45 years of age
  • Career gap of at least one year
